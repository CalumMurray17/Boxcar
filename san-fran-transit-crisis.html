San Francisco Transit

What is MUNI?

The Basics

  • SF's MTA equivalent — covers 46 square miles
  • 83 total lines across all modes
  • Operates buses, trolleybuses, light rail, historic streetcars & cable cars

Light Rail (Muni Metro)

  • 7 light rail lines
  • 117 stations
  • 38.9 miles across the city
  • 5th busiest light rail system in the US

Key Bus Routes

  • 38 Geary — highest ridership bus in SF
  • 14 Mission — essential for late-night commuting
  • 22 Fillmore — key north-south connector
  • 49 Van Ness/Mission — north-south transit spine

Iconic Services

  • Cable cars — Powell-Hyde, Powell-Mason, California St
  • F Market & Wharves — Castro to Fisherman's Wharf
  • Both at risk under severe budget-cut scenarios
San Francisco Transit

What is BART?

Service Area

Serves the broader SF Bay Area across Alameda, Contra Costa, San Francisco, San Mateo, and Santa Clara counties.

The System

5 rapid transit lines · 50 stations · 131 miles of track · Fleet of 805 total vehicles · 7th busiest rapid transit system in the US.

Yellow — Antioch → SFO/Millbrae Blue — Dublin → Daly City Orange — Richmond → San José Green — Daly City → San José Red — Richmond → Millbrae

Note: Red and Green lines do not run after 9pm under current schedule.

Starting January 2027

BART Service Cuts

Train Service

  • 63% fewer service hours
  • Red and Green lines eliminated
  • Trains run every 30 minutes
  • Service ends at 9pm

Station Closures

  • Phase 1: 10 lowest-ridership stations closed
  • Phase 2: Up to 15 stations
  • Up to 25% of track miles removed

Cost & Pricing

  • Fares and parking up to 50% higher
  • Average fare rises to $7.26

Regional Impact

  • Bay Bridge traffic could increase 73%
  • Riders shift back to driving
  • Worsening congestion across the region
Service Reductions

MUNI: Impact on Riders

Route Eliminations

  • Metro wait times doubled
  • Off-peak service (9pm–midnight) cut by up to 60%

Historic Service Gone

  • Cable cars suspended entirely
  • F Market & Wharves reduced or eliminated

Vulnerable Riders Hit Hardest

  • Fare discounts for low-income, seniors, youth & disabled may be cut

City-Wide Fallout

  • Essential workers (restaurant, security, nurses) unable to commute
  • Tourism economy impacted by cable car & F-line cuts
