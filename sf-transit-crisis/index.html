Executive Summary

SF's two major transit systems are facing a structural funding crisis that began with COVID and has never recovered — creating both urgency and opportunity.

San Francisco Transit

What is MUNI?

The Basics

  • MTA equivalent of San Francisco
  • Covers 46 square miles
  • 83 total lines across all modes

Modes of Transit

Operates buses, trolleybuses, light rail, historic streetcars, and cable cars.

Light Rail (Muni Metro)

  • 7 light rail lines
  • 117 stations
  • 38.9 miles across the city
  • 5th busiest light rail in the US

Key Context

Muni is to San Francisco what the MTA is to New York — except the stakes are higher per rider. 38% of Muni riders are low income, meaning they have no car and no backup plan if service is cut.

What Went Wrong?

The pandemic broke a funding model that was already fragile — and the riders haven't come back.

Root Cause

What Went Wrong?

$307M

The Core Problem

Ridership never fully recovered after COVID. Remote work permanently reduced peak commuter demand — the exact segment both systems depended on. BART was covering over 70% of its operating budget from fares before the pandemic.

Potential Fix: Two November 2026 ballot measures — a regional 5-county sales tax and an SF-specific parcel tax — could help close funding gaps. If either fails, cuts start fast.

Impact on Riders

The proposed cuts would reshape daily life for hundreds of thousands of Bay Area commuters.

Service Reductions

MUNI: Impact of Service Reductions

Route Eliminations

  • Metro wait times doubled
  • Off-peak service (9pm–midnight) cut by up to 60%

Historic Service Gone

  • Cable cars suspended entirely
  • F Market & Wharves historic streetcar line reduced or eliminated

An aging MUNI fleet at risk under budget cuts

Vulnerable Riders Hit Hardest

Fare discounts for low-income, seniors, youth, and people with disabilities may be cut.

City-Wide Fallout

Essential workers — restaurant staff, security guards, night nurses — would struggle to commute.

Starting January 2027

BART: Service Cuts

Train Service

  • 63% fewer service hours
  • Red and Green lines eliminated
  • Trains run every 30 minutes
  • Service ends at 9pm

Station Closures

  • Phase 1: 10 lowest-ridership stations closed
  • Phase 2: Up to 15 stations
  • Up to 25% of track miles removed

Cost & Pricing

  • Fares and parking up to 50% higher
  • Average fare rises to $7.26

Regional Impact

  • Bay Bridge traffic up to 73% higher
  • Riders shift back to driving
  • Worsening regional congestion
